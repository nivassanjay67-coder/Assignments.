About Hyundai Company

Hyundai is a South Korean multinational automotive manufacturer, part of the larger Hyundai Motor Group, that produces cars, SUVs, and electric vehicles under the Hyundai and Genesis brands. Founded in 1967, it is a global leader in the automotive industry, known for its innovation in autonomous driving, connectivity, and hydrogen-powered vehicles, and aims to provide mobility solutions with its brand vision of "Progress for Humanity".

Key Aspects of Hyundai Motor Company

  • Origins: Founded in 1967 in South Korea by Chung Ju-Yung, the company has grown from its roots in the country's automobile industry.
  • Global Presence: Hyundai Motor Company has a global network of production bases and exports vehicles to over 200 countries worldwide.
  • Brands: In addition to the main Hyundai brand, the group also operates the luxury brand Genesis.
  • Innovation & Future Mobility: The company is actively involved in developing future mobility technologies, including a focus on autonomous driving, connectivity, and mass-producing hydrogen-powered vehicles.
  • Sustainability: Hyundai Motor Company has a global brand vision of "Progress for Humanity" and seeks to offer sustainable and green manufacturing operations.
  • Parent Company: Hyundai is part of the broader Hyundai Motor Group, a global corporation with a diverse portfolio that includes other industries such as construction, steel, and finance.
